Holi Festival

Holi, known as the "Festival of Colors," is a vibrant and joyous celebration deeply rooted in Hindu culture. It typically takes place in March, marking the end of winter and the arrival of spring. The festival is a two-day affair, with the main festivities occurring on the second day known as Dhulandi or Rangwali Holi. Symbolically, Holi represents the triumph of good over evil, with various mythological stories and legends associated with it, the most famous being the tale of Prahlad and Holika.

History of Holi Festival

One of the most prominent legends associated with Holi is the story of Prahlad and Holika from Hindu mythology. According to the mythological texts, Prahlad was a devout follower of Lord Vishnu, but his father, King Hiranyakashipu, was a tyrant who despised Vishnu. Hiranyakashipu's sister, Holika, possessed a boon that made her immune to fire.
